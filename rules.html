Official Anti-Ragging Guidelines & Penalties

These rules are in strict compliance with the UGC (Prevention and Prohibition of Ragging in Higher Educational Institutions) Regulations, 2009. Ignorance of the rules will not be excused.

What Constitutes Ragging? (Definition)

Ragging involves any act that:

  • Causes or is likely to cause fear, apprehension, or shame or embarrassment to a student.
  • Indulges in rowdy or undisciplined activities.
  • Involves abuse, assault, threats, or criminal intimidation.
  • Compels a student to do any act which they would not ordinarily do and which causes or is likely to cause mental or physical harm.
  • Involves sexual abuse, voyeurism, or indecent exposure.

Disciplinary Actions

Any student found guilty of ragging, even if the victim does not register a formal complaint, shall face **immediate** and **severe** penalties, including:

  • Suspension: Suspension from attending classes and academic privileges for a specified period.
  • Withholding Results: Withholding the result of the student concerned.
  • Fine: Imposition of a fine of up to **INR 50,000**.
  • Expulsion: Expulsion from the institution and debarring from admission to any other institution for a period of up to three years.
  • Criminal Prosecution: Filing a complaint with the police and potential prosecution under criminal law.

Reporting & Protection

We guarantee **confidentiality** and **protection** to the complainant and any witnesses. Reporting can be done anonymously using the **Report** form. Retaliation against any person reporting an incident is strictly prohibited and constitutes a separate punishable offense.
